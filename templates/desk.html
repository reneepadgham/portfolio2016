This is my desk.

There are many like it, but this one is mine. It’s pretty minimal, but it gets the job done.

Empathize

I start work by putting on my empathy hat. I like to envision a day in the life of my user and feel their pain points. I create personas that allow me to understand the different types of experiences that might bring a user to my product.

Research

I use resources such as customer support and user interviews to build out my understanding of the user and the problem. If I have access to quantitative data, I also invite it to the party.

Sketch

I then invite my oldest friends, Pencil and Paper, over. We have a tea party and get the basics together in a flow that can be shown to users and internal teams for feedback. I often invite prototyping tools or code to join.

Polish

Before we go out, I dress up my work with my new friend Sketch - but sometimes reach out to my old friend Adobe. I also like to bring my designs to life using html, css, and javascript.

Test

I have good ideas, but I don’t always know which is best. I like to dress up my work in a few different options and get feedback. This process, called A/B testing, helps me figure out which version is best.

Ship

With that information, I create a finished version. Then, I work with development to bring it to life and ship it off.
