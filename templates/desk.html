Empathize

When I come to my desk for a new project, I start by putting on my empathy hat. To me, this is one of the most valuable parts of my design process. I like to envision a day in the life of my user and feel their pain points. I create personas that allow me to understand the different types of experiences that might bring a user to my product. This process allows me to understand my users and create the best possible experiences for them.



Research

To solidify my understanding, I use resources such as customer support or sales teams and user interviews to help build out my understanding of the user. If the client has been tracking any quantitative data, I also invite it to the party. This gets my bachelor’s in Quantitative Methods in Economics and Mathematics really excited.



Brainstorm/Prototype

After I have a concrete understanding of the problem and the user - I invite my oldest friends, Pencil and Paper, over. We have a tea party (Earl Grey ftw), and after a few rounds, we get the basics together in a flow that can be shown to users and internal teams for feedback. Sometimes I never leave Paper, but I like growing the party so I often invite Invision, one of Invision’s close relatives, or HTML/CSS into the mix depending on the vibe.



Polish

Once I have the core functioning, it’s time to prepare it for presentation to the world. I normally dress it up with my new friend Sketch, but sometimes I reach out to my old friends Illustrator and Photoshop. I also like to bring my design to life using html, css, and javascript. If I don’t do that myself, I like being able to work with the development team making it happen.



Test

During this time, I like to dress it up a few different ways and get user feedback. This process, called A/B testing, helps me figure out which version solves my user’s problems the best.



Ship

With that information, I polish it up and help my team ship it off.
